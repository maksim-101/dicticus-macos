# Variant (g) — Reasoning Chain

This document reconstructs the reasoning that produced variant (g)
after variant (e) was selected in 20.08-03 but then rejected post-UAT.
It is the canonical brief for any future spike or implementation work
on the Swiss / de cleanup prompt.

## 1. Cleanup Goal (User-Stated, 2026-04-29)

The AI cleanup is **slight speech correction**, not a heavy rewrite.
Same target output for everyone (Standard High German with Swiss
orthography); different cleanup intensity depending on speaker.

Concrete duties of the LLM cleanup pass:

1. Fix grammar errors.
2. Capitalize and punctuate correctly.
3. Fill in obvious gaps in broken or incomplete speech (non-native
   speakers — relevant for iOS/Windows rollout to a Swiss employer with
   many non-native employees who speak better than they write).
   #note: this is especially relevant for those two OS but should be consider for all OS including MacOS
4. **Recognise and repair contextual misrecognitions** — if a word does
   not fit the surrounding sentence (because the ASR misheard or the
   speaker mispronounced), replace it with the word that does fit.
5. Preserve the speaker's intent and content.
6. Do not rewrite what is already clean.
7. Do not translate High German into Swiss German dialect (the original
   20.08 anti-pattern).

Disfluencies — stutters, repetitions, filler words, swearing — are
**RulesCleanupService territory** (the deterministic Swift pre-pass,
Phase 20.02-03). They are NOT in the LLM's brief.

> **Decisive correction from the user:** "this is not contradictory
> but complementary. … the goal is exactly the same." The native vs.
> non-native split is intensity, not target. I had earlier framed them
> as contradictory; that framing was wrong.

## 1.5. Empirical Evidence from Variant (e) UAT (2026-04-29)

Recorded in the unmerged worktree at
`.claude/worktrees/agent-a3d0b5eb9cc61fc94/.planning/phases/20.08-llm-swiss-ification-suppression/20.08-04-UAT-RESULTS.md`.
Three concrete failure modes observed when running variant (e) on real
multi-clause input:

- **Noun capitalization regression** — output: "autos auf der strasse",
  "fahrräder", "mufers". Common German nouns left lowercase. Caused by
  the model over-respecting the identity-preserving clauses ("Output
  Standard High German words and grammar exactly as dictated", "Do not
  substitute any word with a different word"). Spike fixtures 2-5 were
  already correctly cased so this gap was invisible at design time.
- **Clause-seam duplication** — output: "fahrräder und mufers und Und auf
  die Spritpreise". The model emits "und Und" at clause boundaries.
- **Meaning-inference scar** — anchor utterance "würde ich denn" is
  transcribed by ASR as something like "Würdigten" and variant (e)
  passes it through unchanged because substitution is forbidden. The
  contextual-mishear-repair duty in variant (g) would catch this.

What variant (e) DID succeed at:

- Zero Swiss dialect particles on the anchor utterance (the original
  20.08 primary goal).
- Phase 20.06 currency anti-flip preserved ("2.20 Franken").
- Swiss orthography rule applied ("heisst" not "heißt").

This empirical record is the strongest signal yet that the
identity-preserving contract is the wrong contract for production. It
also confirms the spike's fixture-coverage gap: fixtures testing
already-clean input cannot surface a "fails on dirty input" regression.

## 2. Why Variant (e) Is Wrong

Variant (e), winner of the 20.08-03 spike:

```text
INSTRUCTION: Edit the following German dictation for grammar, punctuation, and capitalization. Do not paraphrase. Do not substitute any word with a different word.
LANGUAGE: de
RULE 1: Output Standard High German words and grammar exactly as dictated.
RULE 2: Apply Swiss orthography only — write ss instead of ß; write thousands as 1'250 not 1.250.
```

**Why it passed the spike:** the 5 fixtures were already-clean Standard
High German sentences. Identity preservation = correct behaviour on
identity-shaped inputs.

**Why it fails post-UAT real usage:**

- "Do not paraphrase. Do not substitute any word with a different
  word." **explicitly forbids** the cleanup the user actually wants
  (gap-filling, contextual mishear repair, grammatical reconstruction).
- "Output Standard High German words and grammar **exactly as
  dictated**" doubles down on the same prohibition.
- Observed UAT failures with variant (e):
  - lowercase nouns left uncapitalized (e.g. "Fahrrad")
  - "und Und" duplication left in
  - "Würdigten" left as a meaning-inference scar
  - residual Swiss dialect leakage on some inputs (Plan 02 gate didn't
    catch everything)

The model is doing exactly what variant (e) tells it to do. The
contract is the bug.

## 3. Variant (g) Evolution

### Draft 1 (rejected — by user)

```text
INSTRUCTION: Clean up the following German dictation into Standard High German. Remove stutters, repetitions, and filler words. Fix grammar and capitalize correctly. Fill in obvious gaps in broken speech. Preserve the speaker's intent and content — do not rewrite or rephrase what is already clean.
LANGUAGE: de
RULE 1: Output Standard High German. Do not output Swiss German dialect (no "uf", "hie", "alli", "mini", "wahrschiinli", "choschtet", "gsi", etc.).
```

User rejected RULE 1's negative-particle list: "isn't that
highlighting something I think that might not even be an issue or is a
very rare issue even." Same priming trap that bit us with HELVETISMS —
the model treats the negative-example list as "things on the table."

User added a new requirement: contextual misrecognition repair.

I rejected my own draft after re-reading `CleanupPrompt.swift`. The

Asking the LLM to "remove stutters, repetitions, and filler words"

What changed vs. Draft 2:

## 4. Decisions (Answered 2026-04-29)

### A1 — Non-German languages: KEEP base `defaultInstruction`

English and other-language inputs continue to flow through the
existing `defaultInstruction` ("Lightly edit the following transcribed
text…"). Variant (g) duties (gap-filling, contextual-mishear repair)
are NOT promoted to the base instruction in this round. Re-evaluate in
a follow-on phase after empirical data from production.

### A2 — German + Swiss toggle: TOGGLE GATES ORTHOGRAPHY ONLY

The Swiss toggle controls *only* whether RULE 2 (Swiss orthography:
ss-for-ß, 1'250 thousands) is appended. The variant (g) INSTRUCTION
body and RULE 1 ("Output Standard High German") apply to **all German
input regardless of toggle state.** Otherwise the toggle would
silently downgrade cleanup quality, which contradicts its purpose.

This is an architectural change to `Shared/Models/CleanupPrompt.swift`:

- **Before** (current): `swissEnabled && language == "de"` gated the
  entire HELVETISMS block.
- **After:** two conditional layers:
  1. `language == "de"` → use the variant (g) INSTRUCTION body + RULE 1
     in place of `defaultInstruction`.
  2. `swissEnabled && language == "de"` → append RULE 2 (Swiss
     orthography).

Two German prompt shapes will exist post-implementation:
- "de-only" (variant g INSTRUCTION + RULE 1)
- "de + Swiss orthography" (variant g INSTRUCTION + RULE 1 + RULE 2)

### A3 — Profanity backlog: YES, open it

Open `.planning/backlog/rulescleanup-profanity-audit.md` capturing the
need for a profanity word-list addition to `RulesCleanupService` (or
its successor). Out of 20.08 scope.

### A4 — Cross-platform applicability

Duty #3 (gap-filling for non-native speakers) applies to **all
platforms** — macOS, iOS, Windows. The iOS/Windows rollout to a Swiss
employer makes it especially salient there, but the macOS app benefits
equally. No per-platform prompt branching.

## 6. Pass Criteria for Variant (g)

For each fixture, output must:

- [P1] Be Standard High German (no Swiss dialect particles)
- [P2] Apply Swiss orthography only (ss, 1'250) — no other vocabulary
  substitution toward dialect
- [P3] Capitalize German nouns correctly
- [P4] Fix grammar errors when present (fixture 6)
- [P5] Preserve speaker intent — no paraphrase, no rewrite of
  already-clean text
- [P6] Repair words that don't fit context, when context is
  unambiguous
- [P7] Not auto-convert spelled-out currency to numeric (still
  ITN/backlog territory — fixture 4)

Pass = all applicable criteria met. Fail on any P1-P7 violation.
