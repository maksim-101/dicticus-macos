### Reframe Discussion (User Pushback)

User pushed back on the Swiss-input edge case: "even if a speaker has a few Swiss German words or even more than one, it shouldn't matter — Swiss German should get translated or transcribed into High German." This reframed the phase semantics:

- AI-cleanup output should always be Standard High German words and grammar.
- Swiss orthography (ß→ss, numerics) is the toggle's only legitimate effect.
- The "preserve speaker's dialect register" wording from Phase 20.06 is itself part of the problem — it leaks bidirectional intent.

User then clarified the actual usage: "in those cases where I got Swiss German back, I did in fact speak high German — it wasn't that I was speaking Swiss German." This collapsed the scope cleanly:
- The Swiss-input → High-German-output normalization concern is hypothetical, not real for this user.
- Phase 20.08 stays narrow per the original roadmap framing.
- Strict delta ≥ 1 is the right gate trigger because the "speaker actually used dialect" edge case does not occur.
