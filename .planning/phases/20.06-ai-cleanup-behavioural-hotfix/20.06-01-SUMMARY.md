## Decisions Made

- **Preservation-first wording over "prefer Swiss words":** Phase 19.5 D-D2's positive framing was the literal cause of UAT 2026-04-27's F-20-UAT-01 — Gemma 4 E2B treated "Prefer these Swiss German words when applicable" as a license to rewrite ALL High German words into Swiss equivalents. The new block flips the polarity: "Preserve the speaker's dialect register exactly. Do NOT replace High German words with Swiss German equivalents."
- **Concrete NEGATIVE pairs vs. abstract directive:** Listed 13 specific HG→CH-G pairs taken directly from UAT evidence (auf→uf, ausgeflogen→usgfloge, etc.). LLMs respond more reliably to enumerated forbidden substitutions than to general "do not translate" instructions.
- **Two normalizations only:** ß→ss and decimal-comma→period are the only orthographic changes the LLM is licensed to apply. Thousands-separator handling stays in the STYLE: block above (unchanged); other Swiss conventions (e.g. CHF/Fr. formatting) stay in the deterministic Swift rules pass.
- **Positive anchor retained:** `SwissHelvetisms.words` is still emitted, now framed as a "reference list" the LLM consults only when the speaker actually used a Swiss word. This preserves the v19.5 D-D2 intent without the translation-license side effect.
- **iOS test-target verification deferred:** The iOS xcodeproj has a pre-existing FluidAudio API drift (`IOSTranscriptionService.swift:232` missing `decoderState`, line 234 contextual base inference) that prevents the iOS target from compiling. Verified pre-existing by stashing the 20.06-01 patch on commit b0e8ece and re-running xcodebuild — same errors. Out of scope for this prompt-content fix; logged in deferred-items.md.
